Gaza's Healthcare Collapse: A Humanitarian Crisis Unfolds

國際新聞

The devastation of Gaza's healthcare system has left an unprecedented crisis, transforming what was once an efficient medical network into a fractured system struggling to serve 2.3 million residents. As of early 2024, merely 14 out of 36 hospitals remain functional, illustrating a severe blow to the region's medical capacity.

Within the surviving facilities, medical personnel operate under severe constraints. Operating theaters function by emergency lighting, vital supplies diminish rapidly, and corridors overflow with patients seeking essential care. The destruction of medical infrastructure has initiated a chain of crises extending far beyond the immediate impact of demolished structures.

Cancer treatments have ceased, premature infants struggle without vital incubators, and individuals with chronic conditions can no longer access routine treatment. Emergency surgical capabilities, previously available across numerous hospitals, are now severely restricted, compelling difficult decisions regarding allocation of urgent care.

The broader implications of this healthcare collapse are equally severe. Mortality rates from typically curable conditions have escalated, while the threat of infectious disease outbreaks intensifies in overcrowded facilities. Mental health services have virtually vanished when psychological trauma is widespread. Maternal and neonatal care, fundamental for any functioning society, operates at minimal capacity.

For ordinary Palestinians, the destruction of local hospitals compels impossible choices. Many must undertake perilous journeys through conflict zones to reach functioning medical facilities, while others postpone vital care due to inherent risks. The few operational hospitals face overwhelming demand, with patients and families converge upon facilities designed for significantly fewer occupants.

文章中文翻譯

加薩醫療系統的毀壞導致了一場前所未有的危機，將曾經高效的醫療網絡轉變為一個支離破碎的系統，艱難地服務著230萬居民。截至2024年初，在36家醫院中僅有14家仍能運作，清楚地展示了該地區醫療能力所受到的嚴重打擊。

在倖存的醫療設施中，醫護人員在嚴格的限制下工作。手術室靠緊急照明運作，重要物資迅速減少，走廊擠滿了尋求基本醫療的病患。醫療基礎設施的破壞引發了一連串危機，其影響遠超過建築物被毀的直接後果。

癌症治療已經停止，早產兒在沒有重要保溫箱的情況下掙扎求生，慢性病患者無法再獲得常規治療。急診手術能力，原本在眾多醫院都能進行，現在卻受到嚴重限制，迫使醫護人員在緊急護理分配上做出困難的決定。

這場醫療崩潰的更廣泛影響同樣嚴重。原本可治癒疾病的死亡率已經上升，而在擁擠的設施中，傳染病爆發的威脅也在加劇。當心理創傷普遍存在時，心理健康服務卻幾乎消失。對任何正常運作的社會來說都是基本需求的產婦和新生兒護理，現在只能維持最低限度的運作。

對普通巴勒斯坦人來說，當地醫院的破壞迫使他們面臨不可能的選擇。許多人必須冒險穿越衝突區才能到達仍在運作的醫療設施，而其他人則因固有風險而推遲重要治療。少數仍在運作的醫院面臨著巨大的需求壓力，病患和家屬湧向這些原本設計容納更少使用者的設施。
